Wedding Venue

The wedding takes place at The Wildflower Barn - Bluebonnet Barn (www.thewildflowerbarn.com) This Hill Country retreat is nestled in the midst of Cedar and Live Oak trees and features an open, country feel perfect for getting hitched on a gorgeous Texas Spring day! There are TWO barns on the property with ample parking for both. Please only park in places designated for BLUEBONNET barn. Signage will be out!

The venue holds up to 120 guests and will have room for dinner, dancing, a s’mores bar over a fire pit, as well as yard games including horseshoes, washers, and possibly some beer pong. Dress should be semi-formal but comfortable, and please wear flat shoes, as the wedding will be almost entirely outdoors!

What else is going on?

Things to do

Although the wedding is in Driftwood, TX (about a half hour South of Austin) travelers from out of state will fly in to the capital city and should take advantage of the endless fun our city has to offer! Here are just a few options:

Dance Lessons at the Broken Spoke: If you’re going to dance in Texas, you’ll need to learn to two-step! Take a trip to Austin’s oldest Honky Tonk and learn how to boot scoot with the best. Free lessons nightly!

Visit a food trailer park: Austin is a city full of amazing food! Don’t let the fact that your cuisine is coming from a vehicle stop you- there are amazing, award-winning fares at the various food trailer parks throughout the city.

Texas State History Museum: You’ve heard all the tall tales and bragging Texans tell, now go see why! Austin is home to one of the largest and most comprehensive collections of historical artifacts, from pre-settlement, through the Texas Revolution and Civil War, all the way to LBJ and beyond.

Round Rock Express Game: Bryce and Jenny love the Texas Rangers, and their development team plays just minutes from their house! Take in a baseball game for as little as $7 per person and cheer on the Express!

Explore the Hike and Bike Trail: One of Austin’s most famous features are the miles of hike and bike trails that weave throughout the urban landscape. The most popular one, the Lady Bid Lake Hike and Bike Trail (Town Lake trail to older natives!) spans 10 miles and loops around the lake that bisects downtown. The wide, crushed granite trail is great for all abilities and is the perfect way to see the city while still getting fresh air.

Stand Up Paddle Boarding: If you’re looking for a way to enjoy the city with a waterfront view an want to try something a little different than canoeing, then it’s time to SUP! Rentals are located all along Lady Bird Lake for hourly rates and the sport is great, even for beginners.

You Didn't Answer My Question.

FAQs

1. My Save the Date says Lampasas, but this is in Driftwood. What happened? After printing, cutting, punching out, stuffing, embossing, and sealing 80 cards, the venue had to change. To cut the environment some slack, we decided not to trash and reprint. Please refer to your invitation and this website for current details!

2. How do I find the Venue? Use Google maps and type in the address:
The Wildflower Barn
www.thewildflowerbarn.com
1770 North Elder Hill Road
Driftwood, TX 78619

When you turn on the county road, keep your eyes open for signs and follow them to Bluebonnet Barn!

3. Are children allowed? Yes! We do not mind if small children attend our wedding or reception. Be aware that alcohol, curse words, and possibly large exotic animals are all going to be present and there will be no on-site child care.

4. Are pets allowed? No, please leave ALL pets at home! Jenny and Bryce are bringing their two dogs and have paid a pet deposit for them, but they are the only approved animals on site. As Charlie Robison would say, “Leave your dogs in the truck, ‘til you leave.”

5. Will drinks be provided? Yes! We will provide an open bar for all guests over the age of 21. The roads to and from the venue are very winding, so if you are driving, please drink responsibly!

6. I drank too much, how will I get home? Please designate a driver if you plan on drinking a lot! The Hill Country has some beautiful drives, but they can be treacherous at night and we want no one drinking and driving! Austin does have Yellow Cab, 512-452-9999 ($60+, one-way), and Uber (around $50, one-way). No cars are allowed to stay overnight at the venue, so please plan accordingly!

7. Am I allowed to smoke? Legally, yes. However, out of consideration for other guests, especially those recovering from cancer, we ask that you please do not smoke at the wedding. If you must smoke, please do so in the parking lot and PLEASE dispose of cigarettes in the appropriate cans. The area is sensitive to wildfires and irresponsibility when it comes to this will not be tolerated.

8. What are we going to eat? Fajitas! Straight from the couple’s favorite Tex-Mex location in Leander, Jardin Del Rey, chicken and beef fajitas will be served with onions, peppers, rice beans, guacamole, salsa, and most importantly: Queso. Desert will be in the form of cupcakes and a make-your-own s’mores bar around a campfire!

9. How late is this party going? All guests not in the bridal party must leave the property by 10pm. There will be a last call made at 9:30pm.

10. What’s the parking situation? There is plenty of parking on site! Guests will be directed where to park with signage and by venue staff. Please NO campers at the venue- the driveway is narrow!

11. What should I wear? The wedding is OUTDOORS in the spring time in Texas. We highly advise light-weight clothing (bring a light jacket for the evening just in case) and FLAT shoes. High heels are highly discouraged. Cowboy boots are best! Dress is semi-formal, thanks!

12. The wildflowers are so pretty! Can I walk through them/take pictures laying in them/pick them? Ask first! (And no picking flowers!) Please keep in mind that this venue prides itself on their stewardship of their natural landscape. Remember, if you tromp on the flowers, they’re not there for the next person to enjoy. There are some approved areas you can walk through for pictures, just ask!

13. Do you have an Instagram tag? Yes! Please, tag your photos #jbandtheweddinggang so we can see them!
