Wedding Venue

The Venue is called “Horseshoe Falls Ranch.” Located just 45 minutes north of Leander, where Jenny and Bryce call home, Lampasas is where they deer hunt in the fall and visit with friends throughout the year, helping with the various ranch needs of their friend Mr. Headge and petting Hugh and Trudy’s goats.

Horseshoe Falls Ranch sits on what was once a Texas State Park built in 1933 by the Ninth Infantry and the Civilian Conservation Corps. It became known as “Gunderland Park” during WWII when it was bought by private buyers and was later renamed Horseshoe Falls Ranch. Today it is an exotic game ranch that is home to Elk, Axis, Fallow, and various other non-native game animals. Some of these critters may pay guests a visit during the wedding, as the land the pavilion sits on is their everyday home.

Directions to Venue:

After the first traffic light, take a LEFT on CR 3010. Follow signs for half a mile to Horseshoe Falls Ranch, on your LEFT. Staff will direct you where to park once you enter the gate. (About 1 hour from North Austin)

Take 190 West to Lampasas. Turn RIGHT on CR 3010 and follow signs to Horseshoe Falls Ranch, on your LEFT. (About 4 hours)

From Terrell: Take 35S to Temple. Take 190 West to Lampasas. Turn RIGHT on CR 3010 and follow signs to Horseshoe Falls Ranch, on your LEFT. (About 3 hours)

What else is going on?

Things to do

Although the wedding is in Lampasas, TX (about an hour north of Austin) travelers from out of state will fly in to the capital city and should take advantage of the endless fun our city has to offer! Here are just a few options:

Dance Lessons at the Broken Spoke: If you’re going to dance in Texas, you’ll need to learn to two-step! Take a trip to Austin’s oldest Honky Tonk and learn how to boot scoot with the best. Free lessons nightly!

Visit a food trailer park: Austin is a city full of amazing food! Don’t let the fact that your cuisine is coming from a vehicle stop you- there are amazing, award-winning fares at the various food trailer parks throughout the city.

Texas State History Museum: You’ve heard all the tall tales and bragging Texans tell, now go see why! Austin is home to one of the largest and most comprehensive collections of historical artifacts, from pre-settlement, through the Texas Revolution and Civil War, all the way to LBJ and beyond.

Round Rock Express Game: Bryce and Jenny love the Texas Rangers, and their development team plays just minutes from their house! Take in a baseball game for as little as $7 per person and cheer on the Express!

Explore the Hike and Bike Trail: One of Austin’s most famous features are the miles of hike and bike trails that weave throughout the urban landscape. The most popular one, the Lady Bid Lake Hike and Bike Trail (Town Lake trail to older natives!) spans 10 miles and loops around the lake that bisects downtown. The wide, crushed granite trail is great for all abilities and is the perfect way to see the city while still getting fresh air.

Stand Up Paddle Boarding: If you’re looking for a way to enjoy the city with a waterfront view an want to try something a little different than canoeing, then it’s time to SUP! Rentals are located all along Lady Bird Lake for hourly rates and the sport is great, even for beginners.

FAQs

1. How do I find the Venue? The Venue address does not work for GPS. Try “Gunderland Park Lampasas, TX” and you’ll get there in no time!

2. Are children allowed? Yes! We do not mind if small children attend our wedding or reception. Be aware that alcohol, curse words, and possibly large exotic animals are all going to be present and there will be no on-site child care.

3. Are pets allowed? No, please leave ALL pets at home! Full disclosure: Yes, the venue allows pets. However, Jenny and Bryce are bringing their two dogs. One is a 12-year old Border Collie who does not suffer puppy-fools and would really rather everyone just pet him. The other is a 4-year old Blue Heeler who will either love your pet and be best friends or send them to the Emergency Vet. That’s a gamble we’d rather not make. As Charlie Robison would say, “Leave your dogs in the truck, ‘til you leave.”

4. Will drinks be provided? Yes! We will provide an open bar of beer and wine for all guests over the age of 21. The venue requires that ALL drinks be served by a TABC certified bartender, so BYOB is absolutely not allowed during the designated event hours. Rules are rules, please tip your bartender.

5. What are we going to eat? Fajitas! Straight from the couple’s favorite Tex-Mex location in Leander, Jardin Del Rey, chicken and beef fajitas will be served with onions, peppers, rice beans, guacamole, salsa, and most importantly: Queso. Desert will be in the form of cupcakes and a make-your-own s’mores bar around a campfire!

6. I drank too much, how am I getting back to my hotel? There is NO taxi company in Lampasas, TX. Please, please designate a driver and drink responsibly.

7. How late is this party going? All guests not in the bridal party must leave the property by 10pm. There will be a last call made at 9:30.

8. What’s the parking situation? There is plenty of parking on site! Guests will be directed where to park with signage and by venue staff.

9. What should I wear? The wedding is OUTDOORS in the spring time in Texas. We highly advise light-weight clothing (bring a light jacket for the evening just in case) and FLAT shoes. High heels are highly discouraged. Cowboy boots are best! Dress is semi-formal, thanks!
